# Digest Template and Scoring

Use this reference when writing the final AI PM daily brief.

## PM Value Scoring

Score each item from 0 to 5.

5 = must include. Directly affects AI product strategy, major competitor movement, model capability shift, pricing/business model, or strong user-behavior signal.

4 = include if space allows. Useful product/market signal with clear implications.

3 = monitor. Interesting but not urgent, or lacks enough evidence.

2 = archive only. Mildly relevant but not worth the daily brief.

1 = skip. Generic hype, social chatter, or unclear relevance.

0 = reject. No source, duplicate, unverifiable, or unrelated.

## Evaluation Questions

Ask these questions for each item:

- What changed?
- Who is affected?
- Is this a product, model, workflow, pricing, UX, adoption, or community signal?
- Does it reveal a competitor direction?
- Does it reveal a user pain or unmet need?
- Does it change what an AI PM should build, prioritize, test, price, or monitor?
- Is the source primary and linkable?

## Writing Rules

- Start with the most decision-relevant items, not the most viral items.
- Keep each item short enough to scan on mobile.
- Use concrete nouns and product language.
- Avoid vague language such as "值得关注" unless followed by why.
- For each included item, always preserve the original URL.
- Keep facts and PM interpretation visibly separate.
- If the source is long-form, summarize the actual insight, not the episode/article structure.

## Chinese Style

Use simplified Chinese. Keep common AI and product terms in English where natural:

- AI, LLM, API, RAG, prompt, agent, workflow, benchmark, eval, pricing, GTM, onboarding, retention, enterprise, open source

Avoid stiff translation. Write like a sharp AI PM briefing another AI PM.
